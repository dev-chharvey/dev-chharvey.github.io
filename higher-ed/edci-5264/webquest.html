Statistics WebQuest

Introduction

The purpose of this activity is to increase your understanding of statistical modeling and data plotting. You will be researching the composition dates of the complete works of the 19th Century romantic composer, Frédéric Chopin. In addition to researching, organizing, and plotting data, you will have the opportunity to reflect on some theoretical questions involving statistics as well.

The Task

Get together in groups of two. You will need to find a list of Chopin's works. Note that the Opus numbers do not directly correspond to composition dates. Your task is to organize his works in chronological order and make statistical inferences based on patterns you see.

Activities:

  1. Find a list of Chopin's complete works and composition dates. Just the year is sufficient. Be sure to order them chronologically.
  2. Make a stem-and-leaf plot of his works, based on year.
  3. Find the five-number-summary: the lower extreme, lower quartile, median, upper quartile, and upper extreme of the data set.
  4. Using the five-number-summary, draw a box-and-whisker plot of the data.
  5. Find the mean, variance, and standard deviation of the data set.
  6. Find the range and mode of the data set.
  7. Draw a histogram using your data from the stem-and-leaf plot. Decide on what type of skew the data has.

Questions:

  • In the area of Statistics, why might we find it useful to organize Chopin's works chronologically?
  • Is this data set a discrete or continuous data set? Explain the difference between the two types.
  • Discuss the advantages of using the median value over the mean value.
  • Using the histogram, how can the median and mode be interpreted graphically?
  • Knowing how to calculate variance and standard deviation is great, but how are they useful? Discuss the importance and meaning of the variance and standard deviation of a data set.
  • What do the stem-and-leaf plot and the histogram have in common? What do they show that the box-and-whisker plot cannot?
  • Explain how one outlier can dramatically affect the range of a data set. How is the range useful in real life?

The Process

  1. In groups of two, one partner should take on the statistical activities while the other partner researches the questions. Each partner should be interacting and discussing their processes with the other partner.
  2. Near the end of the class period, both partners should be familiar with how to complete the activities as well as the findings to the questions.
  3. Each group will turn in their notes discussing what processes they used to complete the activities. Show your work.

Resources

Learning Advice

Each group is to jot down notes and findings as they are working. These notes will be turned in, so make sure they are copious. However, quality is more important than quantity. It is more important to obtain the big ideas and concepts involved, and not to focus on the small details. With the number of resources at your disposal, you do not have enough time to read them thoroughly. Observe the titles, subtitles, pictures and captions, and diagrams. It is sufficient to skim long texts for main ideas. Feel free to venture outside the given sources. Do cite any new sources you come across, if you are using important information from them.

Conclusion

This WebQuest should have helped you gain better insight on the usefulness and practicality of statistics and data organizing, as well as practice with data plotting and making statistical inferences. In addition to the statistical aspect, this activity should have enhanced your research skills and your ability to extract, organize, and summarize information from a multitude of resources.
