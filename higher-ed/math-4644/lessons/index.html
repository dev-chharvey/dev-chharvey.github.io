The assignment is to complete an online activity in the subject of Statistics. Students will have to do extensive (not intensive) research and answer a series of questions.

What is a paradox?

No, it is not a team of two doctors. A paradox is a logical impossibility that is derived from seemingly acceptable propositions. The following descriptions are examples of something called a Catch-22, a specific type of paradox in which one object is needed to obtain another object, which in turn, is needed to obtain the original object.

You lock your keys in your car. In order to get your car key, you must unlock your car, for which you need the key. What do you do?
In order to find a job, you must have experience in the work field. In order to get experiene in the field, you have to have a job. Where do you start?
Which came first: the chicken or the egg? Chickens can only be hatched from eggs, which can only be laid by chickens.

Another type of paradox, called a self-reference, is illustrated by the following sentence. Can you explain what is going on?

This statement is false.

To find out about some more famous historical mathematical paradoxes, see Zeno's Paradoxes.
