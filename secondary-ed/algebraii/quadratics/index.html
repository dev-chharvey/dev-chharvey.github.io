Quadratics Unit Overview

Updated December 16, 2012.

The quadratics unit focuses on the manipulation of second-degree polynomials, quadratic equations and their real and complex solutions, and characteristics of quadratic functions. Also covered lightly is the Complex number system.

Learning Objectives

Complex Numbers

  • Simplify a square root of a negative number in the domain of complex numbers.
  • Simplify a power of \imath greater than 1.
  • Perform operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication) on complex numbers.
  • Divide complex numbers, and conjugate and simplify a complex fraction.
  • Identify field properties of the complex number system.

Quadratic Expressions and Equations

  • Factor a quadratic trinomial with a leading coefficient equal to, or not equal to, one; and with a positive or negative constant term. Special cases will include perfect squares and differences of squares.
  • Algebraically convert between a quadratic expression's factored form, vertex form, and standard form by expanding, factoring, and completing the square.
  • Find the real solution(s) of a quadratic equation in factored form.
  • Find the real or complex solution(s) of a quadratic equation in vertex form.
  • Find the real or complex solution(s) of a quadratic equation in standard form using the Quadratic Formula.
  • Determine the number, type, and multiplicity of solutions of a quadratic equations using the Discriminant.

Quadratic Functions

  • Given a quadratic function in factored form, vertex form, or standard form: by graphing and algebraically, find the:
    • Roots
    • Vertex
    • y-Intercept
    • Line of Symmetry and Range
  • Describe and apply transformations of quadratic functions in vertex form.
