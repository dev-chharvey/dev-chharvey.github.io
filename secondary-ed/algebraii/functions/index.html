Learning Objectives

Relations and Functions

  • Distinguish between relations and functions, and determine whether a function is one-to-one or not.
  • Evaluate a function at a point and find an input for a given output.
  • Describe properties of functions including:
    • domain and range
    • zeros and y-intercepts
    • turning points (vertices)
    • absolute maximums and minimums
    • intervals increasing or decreasing
    • end behavior
  • Identify parent functions from an equation or a graph, and categorize functions into families based on parent functions.

Combined Functions

  • Perform operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication) on functions.
  • Find the composition of two functions.
  • Identify, graph, and determine a function's inverse, and explore the relationship between inverse functions.
  • Describe transformations of functions in terms of translations (slides), reflections (flips), and dilations (stretches), and represent these transformations with algebraic operations.
  • Identify the transformation of a function by a table, graph, or equation.
