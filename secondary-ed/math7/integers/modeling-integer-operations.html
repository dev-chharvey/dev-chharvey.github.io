Modeling Integer Operations

Updated September 04, 2013.

This table describes Standard 7.3a in further detail.

Operation Two-Colored Counters Algeblocks Number Line
Description of Manipulative

Yellow/white counters represent positive integers; red/black counters represent negative integers. A zero pair consists of one yellow and one red. When put together, they cancel out. The linear ordering on \ZZ is represented by quantity.

The area on the face of a green cube represents an integer. Each cube is one unit. Units placed on the dark side of the mat represent negative integers; units placed on the light side represent positive integers. A zero pair consists of one cube on the dark and one cube on the light. When put together, they cancel out. The linear ordering on \ZZ is represented by quantity.

A number line is a geometric representation of \ZZ. Along the line, the integers are spaced evenly in increasing order. The linear ordering on \ZZ is represented by direction.
Addition

a+b is modeled by adding b chips to the a chips already present on the mat. After cancellation, the remaining chips is the sum.

a+b is modeled by adding b blocks to the a blocks already present on the mat. After cancellation, the remaining blocks is the sum.
Subtraction

a-b is modeled by taking away b chips from the a chips already present on the mat. (If not enough are available, add sufficient zero pairs.) After cancellation, the remaining chips is the difference.

a-b is modeled by taking away b blocks from the a blocks already present on the mat. (If not enough are available, add sufficient zero pairs.) After cancellation, the remaining blocks is the difference.
Multiplication

a\cdot b is modeled by b groups of a chips. The total number of chips is the product. (A negative group is represented by flipping all chips in the group.)

a\cdot b is modeled by a rectangle with width a and height b columns. The area of the rectangle is the product.
Division

Repeated subtraction: a\div b is modeled by a chips separated out into groups containing b chips each. The number of groups is the quotient.

Divvy up: a\div b is modeled by a chips separated out into b groups. The number of chips contained in each group is the quotient.

a\div b is modeled by a rectangle with area a and width b. The height of the rectangle is the quotient.
